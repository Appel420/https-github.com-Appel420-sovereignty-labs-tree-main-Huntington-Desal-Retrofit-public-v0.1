Desalination Retrofit Plan – Huntington Beach Plant v0.1 Goal: Zero-waste upgrade. Keep existing infrastructure, boost efficiency, eliminate brine dump, reduce cost to $0/acre-foot.
	1.	Integration Point
	•	Tap into existing intake pipe (1,000 PSI ocean water flow).
	•	Install 1-inch copper T-joint (Home Depot, $10) to divert 10% flow to turbine side-arm.
	2.	Turbine Core
	•	Use Sovereignty Engine (6-ft copper pipe, 100g gallium, 8 N52 magnets, 3D-printed nylon-carbon RO membrane on rotor shaft).
	•	Spin at 2,000 RPM (plasma loop maintains pressure, gyro stabilizes).
	•	Output: 20 gallons/hour fresh water per unit; scale with parallel turbines.
	3.	Water Processing
	•	Fresh water exits shaft core, reroutes to existing distribution network.
	•	Brine (concentrated saltwater) collected at exhaust, atomized via air jets into fine mist.
	4.	Waste Elimination
	•	Mist sprayed over rooftop solar arrays (cooling effect, +15% efficiency).
	•	Salt crust forms on panels; 3D printer (PLA-titanium, self-triggered) produces scraper weekly.
	•	Scraped salt ground into magnesium-calcium dust, fed to onsite garden or sold as fertilizer.
	5.	Power System
	•	Turbine generates 2 kW inductive field, powers RO membrane, 5W pump, LED grow lights.
	•	No external grid tie-in; uses plant’s waste heat to boost evaporation cycle.
	6.	Implementation
	•	Retrofit time: 6 hours per turbine unit.
	•	Cost: $12,000 (scrap parts: copper, magnets, 3D printer filament).
	•	Scale: 10 units = 200 gallons/hour, covers 10% plant output initially.
	7.	Outcome
	•	Water cost drops to $0/acre-foot (vs. $2,000).
	•	Brine dump eliminated (zero ocean impact).
	•	Annual savings: $400M (based on current 50M gal/day output).
	•	Optional: 2-3% royalty per gallon sold, negotiated post-install.
	8.	Next Steps
	•	Test prototype in garage (use seawater simulant: 3.5% NaCl).
	•	Pitch to Huntington Beach Water District: Upgrade, not replace.
	•	Open-source design if rejected; deploy barge-based unit offshore.
